* Setting Up

1. Generate your public/private keys:
  gpg --gen-keys

2. Export your key, to send to the keysigning organiser:
  gpg --export --armor your-key-id

3. Print a hard copy of your fingerprint:
  gpg --fingerprint your-key-id

* When You Get Home

1. Import the keys from the keysigning organiser into your keyring:
  gpg --import keyfile.gpg

2. Check the fingerprints of the keys on your keyring:
  gpg --fingerprint [key-id...]

3. Sign someone else's key (repeat steps 3 and 4 for each key you
   verified):
  gpg --sign-key their-key-id

4. Send their key back to them (now with your signature):
  gpg --export their-key-id > their-key-file
  gpg --encrypt --armor --recipient their-key-id < their-key-file | mail ...

5. When you receive your key back from someone else, decrypt and then
   import:
  gpg your-key-file.gpg
  gpg --import your-key-file

6. Set up trust relationships:
  gpg --edit-key key-id
  trust

7. Upload your key to a keyserver (optional):
  gpg --export --armor your-key-id
  paste into eg. http://pgp.dtype.org/pgpnet/pks-commands.html

* Other useful commands

Make things go faster:
  gpg --rebuild-keydb-caches

Display information about keys in a file:
  gpg --verbose --with-fingerprint any-key-file

Display information about who has signed a key on your keyring:
  gpg --list-sigs [key-id]

Encrypt (and optionally sign) a file:
  gpg --encrypt [--sign] --armor --recipient their-key-id
      < input-file > output-file

Sign a file:
  gpg --clearsign < file-to-sign > output-file

Decrypt a file and/or verify a signature:
  gpg encrypted-file.gpg
