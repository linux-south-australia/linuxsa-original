The Red Hat Linux Commercial Support Program

Free Public Presentation by
Robert Hart

Manager, Support Services, Red Hat Software Inc.

Tuesday, 16th December, 1997 at 7:00pm

Digital Equipment Corporation
2nd Floor, 139 Frome Street
Adelaide, South Australia

Robert is about to leave Australia to take up a permanent position at Red Hat Software Inc. in North Carolina as Manager, Support Services. This follows an extensive consulting contract from Robert's company (InterWeft - http://www.interweft.com.au) with Red Hat Software during 1997 developing the commercial support program.

Robert comes from an educational background, and this experience is proving useful in developing the Red Hat Certification program. This program is designed to provide one of the quality assurance requirements for the devolved Red Hat commercial support program; skilled and knowledgeable staff at the support partners.

Robert has been using Linux since kernel 0.99 and has used Red Hat Linux since its first release in October 1994 (code named ``Halloween'').

Over the last seven years Linux has become an important operating system in the commercial marketplace, in terms of features, stability, speed and also of verified volume shipments. In the past, the lack of formal Linux support arrangements has been cited as a reason for not using Linux. With the growing use of Linux in the corporate environment, and the increasing demand for commercial support, Red Hat Software has responded by developing a cooperative vendor-backed support system for Red Hat Linux.

Robert will discuss the Red Hat Commercial Support program; how it will work, and how it will be useful to companies requiring vendor-backed support for Red Hat Linux, as well as its advantages to the general Linux community.

LinuxSA is pleased to offer this lecture, and looks forward to the participation of the local computing community. LinuxSA would like to thank Red Hat Software for sponsoring Robert's visit to South Australia.
